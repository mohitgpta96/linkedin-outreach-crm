# Messaging Playbook — LinkedIn Outreach

## Core Philosophy
Every message must answer one question from the prospect's POV:
"Why should I care about this person right now?"

If your message doesn't answer that, delete it and start over.

---

## Tone Guidelines

### BE This
- **Peer-to-peer**: You're a fellow professional, not a vendor begging for work
- **Curious**: Ask questions that show you've done homework
- **Specific**: Reference their actual work, posts, company milestones
- **Generous**: Lead with value — share an insight, resource, or observation
- **Concise**: Respect their time — every word must earn its place

### NEVER Be This
- Salesy or pitchy (especially in connection requests)
- Generic ("I help companies scale their teams...")
- Desperate ("Would love any opportunity to chat...")
- Over-flattering ("Your profile is SO impressive...")
- Robotic ("I am reaching out because I came across your profile...")

---

## Message Structures

### 1. Connection Request (HARD LIMIT: 300 characters)

**Formula:**
[Specific observation about them] + [Why it caught your attention] + [Simple connect ask]

**Example — Good:**
"Saw your team shipped the new payment module in 6 weeks — that's serious execution speed. I run projects in similar fast-ship environments. Would be great to connect."

**Example — Bad:**
"Hi, I'm a project manager with experience in Agile and Scrum. I help companies deliver projects on time. Let's connect!"
(Why bad: generic, self-focused, says nothing about THEM)

**Example — Good:**
"Your post on managing remote dev teams across timezones resonated — dealing with the same IST-EST coordination challenges. Happy to exchange notes."

**Example — Bad:**
"I noticed you work at [Company]. I have extensive experience in project management and would love to explore synergies."
(Why bad: "I noticed" opener, generic, "synergies" is banned)

---

### 2. Follow-Up Day 3 (After Connection Accepted)

**Formula:**
[Thank for connecting] + [Reference something specific from their profile/posts] + [Share a genuinely useful resource or insight] + [NO ask — pure value]

**Example:**
"Thanks for connecting! I saw your team is scaling from 8 to 20 engineers — that's an exciting but tricky inflection point. I put together a lightweight sprint structure that worked well for a similar scaling situation. Happy to share if useful — no strings attached."

**Rules:**
- Do NOT pitch your services here
- Do NOT ask for a call
- DO share something they can actually use
- DO make it clear there's no catch

---

### 3. Follow-Up Day 7

**Formula:**
[Brief callback to Day 3 value] + [Offer something concrete: audit, template, checklist] + [Soft CTA — easy to say yes to]

**Example:**
"Hope that sprint template was useful. One more thing — I built a project health scorecard that CTOs at similar-stage startups have found handy for board updates. Takes 5 mins to fill in. Want me to send it over?"

**Rules:**
- Reference the value you already gave
- Offer something NEW (not a repeat)
- The CTA should be low-commitment (not "let's hop on a call")
- If they haven't responded to Day 3, this is your last unprompted message

---

### 4. Value Offer Message (Only After Engagement)

**Formula:**
[Acknowledge their specific pain point from conversation] + [How you've solved it before — briefly] + [Concrete offer with scope] + [Easy next step]

**Example:**
"You mentioned the sprint planning is eating 4+ hours weekly — that's a common scaling pain. I helped a Series B fintech cut that to 90 mins by restructuring their backlog workflow. I could do a quick 30-min async audit of your current process and share 3 specific fixes. Free, no commitment — I do these to build relationships. Interested?"

**Rules:**
- ONLY send this after they've engaged (replied, liked, commented)
- Keep the offer specific and bounded (not "let's work together")
- "Free audit" or "quick review" works — it's low risk for them
- Always give them an easy out

---

## Words and Phrases

### BANNED (Never Use)
- "Synergy" / "leverage" / "circle back" / "touch base"
- "I help companies..." (generic opener)
- "Would love to pick your brain"
- "I came across your profile"
- "I noticed you..." followed by something obvious from their headline
- "Let me know if you'd be open to..." (passive, vague)
- "Just checking in" (empty follow-up)
- "Quick question" when it's actually a pitch
- "No worries if not" (undermines your value)

### USE THESE INSTEAD
- Their specific project/product names
- Metrics from their public posts or company announcements
- Genuine questions about challenges they've mentioned
- "I built/used/tested..." (shows you do, not just talk)
- References to shared experiences (tools, methodologies, industries)
- Concrete numbers: "cut sprint planning from 4 hrs to 90 mins"

---

## Personalization Requirements

Every message MUST include at least ONE of:
1. A specific project or product they've worked on
2. A post or article they've published (with your take on it)
3. A company milestone (funding, launch, hiring spree)
4. A shared challenge based on their role/industry
5. A mutual connection or community reference

If you can't find any of these, the prospect hasn't been researched enough. Go back and research more before drafting.

---

## Response Handling

### If They Reply Positively
- Respond within 24 hours
- Match their energy and length (don't over-reply)
- Move toward a specific next step (async audit, resource share, short call)

### If They Reply With a Question
- Answer directly, don't dodge into a pitch
- Use the question as a chance to demonstrate expertise
- THEN offer relevant help

### If They Reply Negatively or "Not Interested"
- Thank them graciously: "Appreciate the honesty — all the best with [project]"
- Do NOT argue, re-pitch, or send follow-ups
- Mark them as "declined" in outreach log — never contact again

### If No Reply After Day 7 Follow-Up
- Stop messaging. Period.
- You can engage naturally with their posts (likes, thoughtful comments)
- Re-approach after 3+ months ONLY if you have genuinely new value
