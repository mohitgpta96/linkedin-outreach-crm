# LinkedIn Platform Rules & Limits

## Why This Doc Exists
LinkedIn actively restricts and penalizes accounts that behave like bots
or spammers. Every message Claude generates must respect these limits.
Violating them risks account restriction, shadow-ban, or permanent ban.

---

## Connection Request Limits

### Weekly Limits
- FREE account: ~100 connection requests per week (soft limit)
- LinkedIn Premium / Sales Navigator: ~150-200 per week
- SAFE target: Stay under 80/week to avoid triggering detection
- Never send more than 25 connection requests in a single day

### Warm-Up Sequence (New or Inactive Accounts)
If the account is new or hasn't been actively outreaching:

- **Week 1**: 5-10 requests/day, max 40/week
- **Week 2**: 10-15 requests/day, max 60/week
- **Week 3**: 15-20 requests/day, max 80/week
- **Week 4+**: Cruise at 15-20/day, stay under 100/week

IMPORTANT: Warm-up is critical. Jumping straight to high volume
on a cold account is the #1 reason for restrictions.

### Connection Request Character Limit
- Maximum: 300 characters (including spaces)
- This is a HARD platform limit — LinkedIn truncates beyond this
- Best practice: Aim for 250-280 characters for safety

## Messaging Limits

### InMail (Premium/Sales Navigator Only)
- Sales Navigator Core: 50 InMail credits/month
- Sales Navigator Advanced: 150 InMail credits/month
- InMails to Open Profiles don't consume credits
- Replied InMails get credit refunded

### Direct Messages (1st-degree connections)
- No hard daily limit, but LinkedIn monitors velocity
- SAFE target: No more than 50 DMs per day
- Space them out — don't blast 50 in 10 minutes
- Mix manual engagement (likes, comments) between message batches

### Follow-Up Cadence
- Day 0: Connection request sent
- Day 1-2: Connection accepted → Wait (don't message immediately)
- Day 3: First follow-up (value message, no pitch)
- Day 7: Second follow-up (offer something concrete)
- Day 7+: STOP if no reply — shift to passive engagement only

## Content Engagement Rules

### Profile Viewing
- Free: 80-100 profile views per day
- Sales Navigator: 150+ per day
- SAFE target: Stay under 80/day
- Note: Excessive profile viewing without connecting looks suspicious

### Post Engagement (Likes, Comments)
- No strict limit, but pattern matters
- Engage with prospect's content BEFORE sending connection request
- Leave thoughtful comments (2+ sentences), not just "Great post!"
- Space engagement across the day — don't like 30 posts in 5 minutes

### Content Publishing
- Posting your own content improves profile visibility and SSI score
- 2-3 posts per week is optimal
- Content about PM insights, project wins, methodology tips works well
- This creates inbound interest (people find YOU)

## Account Health Indicators

### Social Selling Index (SSI)
LinkedIn scores accounts from 0-100 on four pillars:
1. Establishing professional brand
2. Finding the right people
3. Engaging with insights
4. Building relationships

Higher SSI = more trust from LinkedIn = higher limits.
Check SSI at: linkedin.com/sales/ssi

### Warning Signs Your Account Is At Risk
- "You've reached the weekly invitation limit" — hard stop
- Connection requests getting ignored at >60% rate
- Multiple "I don't know this person" flags
- Sudden drop in profile views or post reach
- Unable to send messages temporarily

### Recovery Actions If Flagged
- STOP all outreach immediately for 72 hours minimum
- Don't withdraw pending requests (looks more suspicious)
- Engage organically: comment on posts, share articles
- Resume at 50% of previous volume
- Increase acceptance rate by improving targeting and messages

## What LinkedIn Considers Spam

### Instant Red Flags
- Sending identical messages to multiple people
- Copy-paste connection requests with no personalization
- Pitching in the connection request itself
- Messaging immediately after connection is accepted (within minutes)
- Using automation tools that LinkedIn can detect
- Sending links in first message (especially to external sites)

### Subtle Red Flags
- Very high volume with low acceptance rate
- Connecting only with people far outside your industry
- Sudden spikes in activity after long dormancy
- Repetitive message patterns (even if slightly varied)
- Connecting and immediately unfollowing

## Compliance Checklist (Run Before Every Batch)

Before generating any outreach batch, verify:

- [ ] Weekly request count is under 80 (or warm-up limit)
- [ ] Daily request count will stay under 20
- [ ] No two messages in the batch are substantially similar
- [ ] Each message has genuine personalization (not just name swap)
- [ ] No pitch or CTA in connection requests
- [ ] No external links in first-touch messages
- [ ] Follow-up timing respects the cadence (Day 3 / Day 7)
- [ ] Account SSI is above 40 (check weekly)
- [ ] Acceptance rate from last batch was above 40%

If acceptance rate drops below 30%, STOP and review:
- Is the ICP wrong? (targeting people who don't care)
- Are messages too generic? (not enough personalization)
- Is the volume too high? (LinkedIn penalizing)

## Platform-Specific Formatting

### Connection Request
- Plain text only (no bold, italic, or links)
- No emojis (looks unprofessional in this context)
- No hashtags
- Must be under 300 characters

### Direct Messages
- Plain text preferred
- Links okay after 2nd message (never in first touch)
- Bullet points okay for sharing structured info
- Keep under 500 words — shorter is almost always better
- Emojis: maximum 1, and only if it fits naturally

### Profile Optimization (Pre-Outreach)
Before starting any outreach, ensure profile has:
- Professional headline mentioning PM expertise and value prop
- About section with clear positioning (not a resume dump)
- Featured section with case studies or testimonials
- Recommendations from past clients/colleagues
- Recent activity (posts, comments) showing expertise
